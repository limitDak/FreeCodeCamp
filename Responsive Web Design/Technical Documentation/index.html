Introduction

Hello! I've dedicated this web page to be my (Dakota) technical documentation page. This page is based off the ruleset that Jan has made for his Hardcore Nuzlockes which you can check out yourself if you'd like. I take no credit whatsoever in the making of this documentation besides formatting it for this website. That being said, I have some additional tips and tools following the documentation which you can check out as well. Hope you enjoy!

What is this documentation?

This is the ruleset I personally use in my Hardcore Nuzlockes that I do on my stream at twitch.tv/PokemonChallenges. To me this represents the perfect balance of challenge and fun, of random and skill elements. This is in no way the be all end all of challenge runs and there are definitely some downsides to this ruleset. It requires a lot of experience as a Nuzlocker, and I do not recommend using this ruleset as your very first Nuzlocke challenge.

Additionally, this ruleset usually requires quite a lot of grinding before gym battles. However, I still believe them to be the baseline for one of the most fun ways ever to play a Pokémon game and I hope you give it a try.

All credit for coming up with the original Nuzlocke challenge goes to its creator. Check out his comics here: nuzlocke.com.

1. Encounters

1.1 Basic Rules of Obtaining Pokemon

Only the first Pokémon I encounter in a specific area may be caught. The area a Pokémon was caught in is defined by the "Met on:" text in its Summary. This includes Pokémon hatched from eggs. Their “Met on:” will display the area they hatched in, making it possible to acquire encounters in places where it is usually not possible to encounter a Pokémon (e.g. in a city).

If the first encounter in an area is killed off in battle, the encounter may not be rerolled (a reroll is considered another chance at an encounter while ignoring and not catching the previous one).

These rules apply as soon as I am first able to encounter and catch a Pokémon. For example, Pokémon I encounter in tall grass before I am able to receive Poké Balls do not count as my first encounter of the Route.

1.2 Methods of Encountering Pokemon

I may choose the method of acquiring the encounter freely. Fishing, surfing, grass, hordes, Rock Smash, honey, Headbutt, etc. are all considered legitimate. The exceptions to this are DexNav, shaking grass and Pokémon obtained in an SOS chain started on the first legal encounter of the route (and all equivalents thereof).

A gift Pokémon is considered a legitimate encounter and will be treated as such. I may not encounter another Pokémon after receiving one as a gift.

1.3 Manipulating Encounters

I may freely choose my starter or any other gift Pokémon that has multiple options (Casino, etc.).

If the first encounter on a route is a double battle or horde encounter, the Pokémon I can catch in that battle may be chosen by me.

Repelling in an area to get a certain encounter (high level, different floor, roaming, or static encounter) is allowed.

1.4 Additional and Illegitimate Encounters

Trading with NPCs is allowed and not considered an encounter. I may trade with NPCs in areas that I have previously received an encounter in or plan to acquire an encounter in.

If I, at any point, encounter a shiny Pokémon during the run, I may catch that Pokémon. If I already have a Pokémon of the same species I may choose to replace it with the shiny Pokémon (Shiny Clause). If the first encounter on a route is a shiny Pokémon, I do not get an additional encounter. I explain my reasoning for this rule in this clip: Shiny Clause.

I may not use two Pokémon of the same species or evolution line (Species Clause). If the first encounter of a Route belongs to an evolution line of a Pokémon that I have already received as an encounter in that run, the encounter on that route may be rerolled until I encounter a Pokémon that I may use. If there are no Pokémon in the area that belong to an evolution line I have not previously caught, I don’t get an encounter in that area. Species Clause persists after death (e.g. if I encounter a Zubat after losing my Crobat, I may not catch the Zubat).

I may decide to ban usage of certain Pokémon depending on the game I am currently playing. For example, Blissey and Salamence are Pokémon I will usually not use, as they make gameplay too easy for my taste. If I encounter a banned Pokémon, I may reroll my encounter for that area. However, a Pokémon from the same evolution line as a banned Pokémon will count as my encounter and I may use it (e.g. I may catch Shelgon, but not evolve it to Salamence).

As a general rule of thumb, I ban legendary and mythical Pokémon, as well as Mega evolved forms. For some pointers on Pokémon I have previously banned on playthroughs see my outdated ban list: http://bans.pchal.tv.

2. Losing Pokemon

2.1 Basic Conditions for Death

If at any point the HP of one of my Pokémon is reduced to 0 that Pokémon is considered dead and may no longer be used. I must permanently put the Pokémon into the PC as soon as possible. I may not take a dead Pokémon out of the PC (unless in the highly specific cases detailed below).

2.2 HM Mules and Forced Reviving

If I need to use a certain HM to progress in the game and none of my alive Pokémon are able to learn it, I may pick up a dead Pokémon from the PC for the sole purpose of teaching it the required HM. This does not include moves like Flash, Thief for stealing Items or using Sweet Scent for hordes. An exception may be made for HM Fly, as it raises the quality of the content I make.

I may catch an additional HM mule if I have no Pokémon dead or alive that can help me progress.

If I end up in a situation where a dead Pokémon or an HM mule is put into battle, I must switch into a living Pokémon immediately. I may not use the moves Assist or Beat Up (or any mechanically similar moves) while I have a revived Pokémon or HM mule on my team. If a dead Pokémon or an HM mule is put into battle and cannot switch out (for example due to the effects of Arena Trap, Mean Look, or variations thereof), this is considered breaking the rules. See 6.2 Rule Breaking for more information.

3. Level Caps

3.1 Basic Rules for Level Caps

Any Pokémon I use for battle may not exceed the next gym leader's highest levelled Pokémon in level. This applies exactly until the gym battle is initiated, so over-levelling during a gym fight is allowed.

The level cap for the Elite Four is to be determined by me. A good starting point is the last Elite Four member’s highest levelled Pokémon. The level cap is in effect until I initiate the first Elite Four battle. I may not use any rare candies after initiating the Elite Four gauntlet.

3.2 Exceeding the Level Cap

If a Pokémon does over-level under any other circumstances not specified above or I catch an over-levelled Pokémon, it is considered dead until I beat the gym that unlocks its level. An over-levelled Pokémon must be put into the PC as soon as possible and may not be taken out (except to use it as an HM mule as specified in 2.2. HM Mules and Forced Reviving).

A Pokémon that exceeds its level cap during a battle or is brought into battle through Roar/Whirlwind/Dragon Tail etc. must be switched out immediately. I may not use the moves Assist or Beat Up (or any mechanically similar moves) with an over-levelled Pokémon on my team. If an over-levelled Pokémon is put into battle and cannot switch out (for example due to the effects of Arena Trap, Mean Look, or variations thereof), this is considered breaking the rules. See 6.2 Rule Breaking for more information.

If at any point all my Pokémon are above the level cap, the run is considered dead and I must restart.

4. Miscellaneous

4.1 Items

At no point may I use non-Poké Ball Items from my bag during a battle. Among others, this includes Healing Items, Battle Items, and Poké Dolls, but does not apply to Items held by my Pokémon.

At no point can more than one Pokémon on my team hold Leftovers.

4.2 Settings

The battle style must always be on “set”.

The Gen 6+ Exp Share may be turned on or off at any time.

When playing on an emulator I may speed up the game at any point.

5. Losing The Run

The run is considered lost when I lose a party of Pokémon and enter the white out screen. This applies even if I have other Pokémon left in the PC.

In a tag battle with an AI partner it is possible to lose all 3 Pokémon I brought to the battle, but still win the fight because my AI partner managed to beat the opponent(s). In this case I do not enter a white out screen and the run is not considered dead. Only the three Pokémon I lost during the tag battle are considered dead.

In Sky Battles (Generation 6) it is possible to lose the battle without entering a white out screen: The opponent can knock out all my Pokémon eligible to participate in a Sky Battle, but I still carry other Pokémon with me. In this case the run will not be considered dead, only the Pokémon I lost during the Sky Battle are considered dead.

6. Crashes and Rule Breaking

6.1 Crashes

In case of an emulator or console crash I must reload the last save and the following rules apply:

  • All encounters I have gotten between the save point and crash are considered lost and may not be rerolled.
  • Any Pokémon that died between the last save point and crash are still considered dead.
  • I may re-battle any required trainer battle that I have beaten between save point and crash. I may use any method I see fit to clear this battle (using dead Pokémon, modifying the save etc.). Any Pokémon that faints during such a re-battle is not considered dead.

6.2 Rule Breaking

If at any point I break one of these rules on accident (or purpose), the standard punishment is to reload at the last save point and kill off the Pokémon that might have been saved by said rule violation.

7. Tips From Dakota

7.1 Pokelink

Pokelink is an extremely useful tool when it comes to playing Nuzlockes. It allows you to keep track of what encounters you have had so far, the current status of your team, the basic metadata for your party Pokemon (Nature, EV's, IV's, etc.) and can even start your emulator with the correct game! I highly recommend this program if you have trouble keeping track of your info during your Nuzlocke.

7.2 General Tips

Some general tips that will help you on your Nuzlocke journey that you might overlook:

  • Check your party's status before going into a fight. It's not uncommon that you'll forget to heal and end up losing a team member for a reason that you could have prevented.
  • It never hurts to check up on trainer teams for a given route, especially when you're playing a game like Emerald Kaizo. Having the advantage of knowing the opponent's team can be the way to victory.
  • Always equip held items before a major battle! Even if it may seem pointless to add a Oran or Sitrus Berry, it's better than that Pokemon not having a held item at all!

7.3 Cheat Codes

It's not uncommon to use cheat codes for Nuzlockes, especially for berries. Since the internal clock doesn't work for the GBA emulators, it's impossible to acquire new berries naturally. On top of that, berries are also tedious to acquire, so I have listed a few cheat codes (specifically for Emerald) for some of the most common berries.

In order to use the codes, enter the code 82005274 0YYY and replace YYY with item digits. Then go to the Poke Mart and purchase the first item in the buy list. The first item is your chosen berry.

Example: Enter code 82005274 0085 for Cheri berry.

085 – Cheri Berry 086 – Chesto Berry 087 – Pecha Berry 08E – Sitrus Berry 08D – Lum Berry
